Civic Reflexivity Engines:
A New Framework for Democratic AI Governance

This paper introduces Civic Reflexivity Engines (CREs) ‚Äî an entirely new institutional form in which algorithmic systems function not as decision-makers, but as instruments of collective self-examination. Rejecting the exhausted binary of "optimise or regulate," the work proposes reflexive co-constitution as its theoretical foundation ‚Äî a paradigm where AI and civic judgment continuously reshape one another, shifting computation from prediction to interrogation, from automation to exposure, and from sovereignty to translation. Democracy is reconceptualised not as a voting mechanism but as a metabolic system capable of ingesting complexity, processing contradiction, and transforming insight into institutional learning. This represents the first comprehensive proposal for reflexivity as an infrastructural layer of governance ‚Äî grounded in Helix Thinking methodology and supported by deployable prototypes from the Kallol Research Hub ecosystem.

World-First Concept Civic Reflexivity Engines ‚Äî a new institutional category with no prior equivalent in governance literature
Original Theory Reflexive Co-Constitution ‚Äî AI and civic judgment continuously co-evolving through structured feedback
Paradigm Shift Democracy redefined as a metabolic system ‚Äî not ritual, not mechanism, but living institutional intelligence
New Methodology Helix Thinking ‚Äî an indigenous analytical framework applied to computational governance architecture
